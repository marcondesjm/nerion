# Research Brief: Instagram Feed Publisher

Prepared: 2026-05-19
Squad: instagram-feed-publisher
Company: NerionOS

## Key Findings

1. Instagram carousels remain a strong feed format for educational and B2B content because they create swipe depth, saves, shares, and longer time on post. Confidence: medium. Corroborated by Sprout Social, Adpicto, and local best-practice files.
2. The first slide is the highest-leverage element. It must carry a bold visual, a clear promise, or a curiosity gap that makes the next swipe feel necessary. Confidence: high. Corroborated by Sprout Social and local Instagram Feed guidelines.
3. High-performing carousels work best when each slide has one job, one idea, and clear visual continuity. Confidence: high. Corroborated by Sprout Social and local Instagram Feed/Image Design guidelines.
4. Useful, reference-worthy formats drive saves: frameworks, checklists, myths, before/after breakdowns, tutorials, and problem-to-solution flows. Confidence: medium. Corroborated by Digital Codex, Sprout Social, and local Instagram Feed guidelines.
5. Publishing must never be fully automatic without explicit final approval. Dry-run, validation, preview, and final confirmation are required before a live Instagram post. Confidence: high. Corroborated by local Social Networks Publishing best practices.

## Recommended Operating Model

1. Collect the user's content focus for the day.
2. Research the topic enough to find one useful insight, trend, or product-growth pain.
3. Generate 3 hook/angle options and let the user choose one.
4. Create the Instagram Feed carousel script using the chosen angle.
5. Ask the user to approve the copy before generating visuals.
6. Design/render the carousel slides at 1080x1440.
7. Review copy, visual clarity, brand fit, platform constraints, and publishing readiness.
8. Present a publish preview and run dry-run before any live post.

## Recommended Carousel Formats For NerionOS

- Problema -> Solução: best for UX/product bottlenecks and SaaS conversion issues.
- Mito vs Realidade: best for provocative, descontraído content that challenges founder assumptions.
- Antes e Depois: best for redesigns, MVP polish, brand/product transformation.
- Tutorial: best for checklists and practical product-building workflows.
- Editorial/Tese: best for market takes and strategic arguments.
